About

I am a web developer, a new media artist, and an arts educator. I create web applications, video installations sound compositions, and deconstruct technological constructs. I love to code, teach code and to empower students and myself through digital agency. One of my goals in life is to never stop learning, and I have found to best way to do that is to strike a balance between input and output.

I haven't totally learned a skill until I can share that skill to others. Using this technique is the foundation to my pedagogies in the class room, and in my professional development. I pride my self on my creative problem solving, and my rapid learning skills. My motto: You will fail to learn if you don't learn to fail.

The Degenerator

Check it out live Check out the code

Final project for the Iron Yard: The Degenerator is a photo sharing experience app where users can upload an image that is affected by each view it receives. Users set the type of degeneration (filters) and the number of views before the photo and its comments are permanently removed from the server. It was created by a team of two: Jeff Minbiole and myself
